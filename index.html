Venture · H-Farm

We chose not to build a medical device. That decision made everything else possible.

Italy has 14.6M people over 65. Most eldercare technology treats them as patients to monitor. R.O.S.S. starts from the opposite hypothesis: the elderly person is not a condition to manage — she's someone to accompany.

€3.8B TAM Italy #1 at H-HACK 2 patents in progress €1,480/yr vs €1,400/mo carer

The insight

The eldercare market is saturated with products the elderly reject — because they feel like surveillance. Our buyer (the adult child) has looked at all of them and excluded them. The gap isn't technology. It's positioning.

"Companion, not device" isn't marketing. It's the foundation of our regulatory strategy (AI Act Art. 50, non-MDR), our IP architecture (zero biometric sensors), and our technical design.

What we built

Local-first architecture: on-device LLM (quantized, LoRA-adapted), speech-to-text and TTS entirely local. The core is a biographical knowledge base — a structured map of the user's people, places, routines, and memories.

Three presence modes (Active, Silent, Reactive). Every day, the family receives a Daily Report — a narrative letter about her day. Not a dashboard. A letter.

My role

I run the commercial and financial engine. Financial models, unit economics, cap table, fundraising structure. Pricing hypothesis: €1,000 device + €40/month — less than one month of a live-in carer.

Market sizing: TAM Italy ~€3.8B (2.55M families), TAM EU-27 ~€23B. I manage investor relations and own the WTP research methodology.

Where we are

AI Build · Sprint

500 pages of technical manuals. Queryable in seconds, by voice.

MANTA Group supplies Leonardo and Boeing. Their operators work the floor with 500+ pages of printed technical manuals — indexed by hand, searched by memory. We had one month to change that.

500+ pages indexed 1-month sprint 12 companies pitched

The problem

Manual lookup on an aerospace floor is slow and breaks flow. The information exists — it's trapped in PDFs. The question was how to make it accessible the way operators actually work: hands busy, eyes on the part.

What we built

A functional RAG-based voice agent. Voiceflow for the voice layer, a RAG pipeline over the full technical documentation. Operators query 500+ pages via voice or text — answer in seconds, not minutes. No new hardware, no retraining.

Led a cross-university team of 4 (H-Farm College × Università di Puglia). Delivered a working MVP in a 1-month sprint. Pitched to a jury of representatives from 12 participating companies.

Why it matters

Voice + retrieval + clean interface can replace workflows that haven't changed in decades. Every knowledge-intensive field where the person doing the work can't stop to type is a version of this problem.

Incubation · H-Farm Startup Centre

My first real startup experience: research, financial model, pitch deck, and an insight about the channel nobody had questioned.

Three months at H-Farm Startup Centre's Pre-Acceleration Program, working on Agromercato.com — a B2B wholesale food marketplace. Pilot: the Mercato Agroalimentare della Sardegna, €150M+ annual turnover.

€150M+ pilot market 3-month program First public pitch

The program

Three months of structured startup methodology — weekly sessions plus 1:1 mentor consultations booked on demand. Everything taught was applied immediately to the real project: market research (qualitative and quantitative), interviews with wholesalers, business model definition, financial model, pitch preparation.

The key insight

Primary research revealed that WhatsApp was already the dominant channel on both sides of the transaction. Wholesalers received orders on it. Buyers sent them on it. Building a new app would mean asking people to change a behaviour that already worked.

The right answer was to make the existing channel intelligent — not to replace it. We designed the concept; we did not build it. The product thinking was the deliverable.

What I took from it

First exposure to the full early-stage methodology, learned by doing. Pitched at the Ecommerce Food Forum in Bologna — first public pitch in front of an industry audience. Delivered a pitch deck to a jury at the end of the program.

Venture Thinking · Live MVP

We killed our first startup when the financial model didn't close. Then we built this.

We started with CheckTheStreet — an urban safety app. Built the product, mapped the personas, designed the B2B2C model. Then ran the financial model: revenues couldn't cover operating costs at 36 months. And the content verification problem — a crowdsourced safety platform in the wrong hands becomes a misinformation weapon. We didn't defend the solution. We questioned the problem.

TAM €5B global 34% ESN integration +10.7% YoY growth Live MVP

The pivot

We asked: who experiences the information gap most intensely? Not the Italian student who knows the city. The international student — arriving with zero local context, zero network, often a language barrier, for 6–12 months. Same problem, more acute, on a ticking clock.

From that question came LocaLì: not a safety tool, but the student layer of the city. Map & Discovery, Student Deals, Safe & Chill.

The problem is real

ESNsurvey XV (2024, N=17,855): only 34% of international students actively integrate with the city they arrive in. 50,000 international students arrive in Italy every year (+10.7% YoY) — all starting from zero. I did Erasmus in Vilnius and navigated an unknown city through fragmented WhatsApp groups. That experience built the first hypothesis.

The model

B2C freemium: free Guest mode → €2.99/month or €9.99/semester. TAM €5B globally, SAM €400M Europe, SOM €40M (Italy beachhead). Live MVP shipped on Lovable.

LocaLì app: map discovery, student deals, pricing tiers.
